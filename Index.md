## The Game in One Paragraph

A sidescrolling rogue-lite set in early Tokugawa Japan. You play a lone ronin tracing Musashi's historical path. The game tracks every act of harm and grace through a hidden moral ledger — never shown to you directly, only felt through how the world responds. Combat registers *how* you fight, not just whether you do. Your greatest rival is shaped by your own choices, becoming your mirror. The game's structure splits in two: a wandering first half where karma accumulates, and a war arc where it settles. The final pre-war duel at Ganryūjima is either a confrontation or a conversation, depending entirely on who you've become.

## Core Design Principles

1. **Karma as deformation, not meter.** The ledger is never shown. Consequences arrive organically.
2. **The body doesn't lie.** Expressive karma (how you fight) outweighs intentional karma (what you say).
3. **The camera is the moral grammar.** Top-down = context. Side-view = body. POV = soul.
4. **The rival is the mirror.** Kojiro reflects who you are. His nature changes as yours does.
5. **Fixed landmarks, variable arrivals.** The events of Musashi's life cannot be skipped. Who you are when you reach them determines everything.
6. **Always keep it personal.** No tactics screens. No war maps. One person's experience, one moment at a time.
